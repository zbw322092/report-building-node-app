# COMPATIBILITY
First is the good part, window.scrollTo and window.scroll have solid performance in browser compatibiliy.


_But no exact browser compatibiliy document found so far._
Sadly, most of native scroll related properties do not has good browser compatibiliy.
scroll-behavior is not support in all IE browsers, including IE Edge. Neither, all of the Safari, iOS Safari and Android webview browsers.
Element.scrollIntoView() is wide BASICALLY support -- working in most of browsers, however, in lots of browsers the configuration options are not working. 
      
        // behavior: "smooth" option is not working in lots of browsers

        element.scrollIntoView({behavior: "smooth"});
Check Details
Method like Window.scrollByPages(), Window.scrollByLines() are even not standard methods.
> This feature is non-standard and is not on a standards track. Do not use it on production sites facing the Web: it will not work for every user.


reference: https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/API/Window/scrollByPages
